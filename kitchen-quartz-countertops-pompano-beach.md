## **Technical Realities (So Your Top Looks Great for Decades)**

### **Thickness: 3cm vs 2cm**

  * **3cm (≈1-1/4 ″)** is Florida’s standard—sturdy with minimal extra support.

  * **2cm (≈3/4 ″)** can work with built-up edges or modern European looks; may need more support.  

### **Overhang & Support**

  * Standard overhang is **1 ″–1.5″**.

  * **> 10″ **[**countertop**](https://www.google.com/maps?cid=2937615344441069978) **overhangs** (like seating) usually require hidden brackets or corbels.

  * We place supports to avoid knee-knocks while protecting the slab.  

### **Seam Strategy**

We minimize seams and place them where pattern alignment is clean (away from
sinks when possible). With consistent quartz patterns, seams practically
disappear.

### **Cutouts & Clearances**

  * **Sinks & Cooktops:** manufacturer templates guide exact cut sizes.

  * **Dishwasher & Range Slots:** We leave expansion room, then finish edges cleanly.

  * **Outlets & Windows:** We check backsplash heights and make smart compromises on sill lines.

## **Care & Maintenance (Easy, Daily Habits)**

### **Daily Cleaning**

Use warm water, mild dish soap, and a soft cloth. For sticky spots, a quick
soak and a non-abrasive pad do the trick.

### **Stain & Etch Reality**

Quartz resists stains exceptionally well. Wipe spills promptly—especially
berries, wine, turmeric—to keep whites bright.

### **Heat & Cutting**

Quartz tolerates brief warmth, but **use trivets** under hot pans and slow
cookers. Always **use cutting boards** —knives can dull and micro-scratch any
surface.

### **What to Avoid**

Abrasive powders, paint strippers, drain openers, and harsh solvents. If
something strong spills, rinse immediately.

## **FAQs**

### **1) How long does a**[ **quartz
countertop**](https://www.google.com/maps?cid=2937615344441069978)
**installation take from order to finish?**

Once you approve your slab and we complete templating, fabrication typically
takes about a week. Most installs finish in a single day. Complex islands,
waterfalls, or full-height quartz backsplashes may add time—we’ll outline this
upfront.

### **2) Can quartz handle hot pots straight from the stove?**

Use trivets. Quartz can withstand brief warmth, but prolonged or extreme heat
can stress the resin binders. Trivets and hot pads protect your investment—and
your finish.

### **3) Will seams be visible in my kitchen?**

We minimize seams and place them thoughtfully. With many quartz patterns,
seams are subtle; with bold veins, we align the pattern for the cleanest look.
We’ll show you proposed seam locations before fabrication.

### **4) What’s the best edge for families with kids?**

Eased or half bullnose edges are excellent—low-profile, comfortable, and easy
to wipe. They also soften accidental bumps.

### **5) Do quartz counters stain or need sealing?**

Quartz is non-porous and does **not** require sealing. It’s highly stain-
resistant—just wipe spills promptly and clean daily with mild soap and water
to keep surfaces bright.
