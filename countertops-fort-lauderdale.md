### **Granite Countertops**

Granite is a natural stone with unique patterns and movement. It’s heat-
resistant and tough, but it typically needs sealing to help prevent staining.

#### **Best For**

  * People who love natural variation  

  * Cooks who want strong heat performance  

### **Marble Countertops**

Marble is stunning—no argument. But it’s softer and more prone to etching and
staining. We often recommend marble for statement areas (like an island) if
you understand the trade-offs.

#### **Best For**

  * High-design kitchens  

  * Homeowners who don’t mind patina and character over time  

### **Quartzite Countertops**

Quartzite is a natural stone (not the same as quartz) with dramatic veining
and excellent durability. It’s a great “beauty + strength” option, though
pricing can vary widely.

#### **Best For**

  * Natural-stone lovers who want durability  

  * High-end renovations

## **Pricing and What Actually Affects Your Countertop Cost**

Let’s talk honestly about pricing—because countertop quotes can feel
confusing.

### **What Impacts Cost the Most**

  * Material selection (quartz vs. natural stone)  

  * Slab size and number of seams  

  * Edge details and cutouts  

  * Backsplash height and complexity  

  * Sink type (undermount, farmhouse, drop-in)  

#### **The “Hidden” Cost Driver**

Seams and layout. A simple design with fewer seams can reduce labor and
improve the final look.

## **FAQs**

### **1) What are the best countertops for a busy kitchen?**

We typically recommend quartz because it’s non-porous, stain-resistant, and
low maintenance—ideal for high-use kitchen countertops.

### **2) Do quartz countertops need sealing?**

No. Quartz is engineered and non-porous, so it doesn’t require sealing like
many natural stones.

### **3) How do I choose the right countertop color?**

We suggest matching your countertop to your cabinet tone, flooring, and
lighting. If you’re unsure, we lean toward timeless: light neutrals with
subtle movement.

### **4) Can I place hot pans directly on quartz?**

We recommend using trivets or heat pads. Quartz is durable, but sudden high
heat can damage the resin binders.

### **5) What makes a countertop quote go up or down the most?**

Material choice, the number of cutouts, edge detail, and seam complexity are
usually the biggest pricing factors.
