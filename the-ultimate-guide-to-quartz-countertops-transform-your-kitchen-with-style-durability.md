## What Are Quartz Countertops? An Overview

### Defining Quartz Countertops

Quartz countertops are engineered surfaces made from natural quartz—one of the
hardest minerals on earth—combined with resins and pigments to create a
durable, versatile material. Unlike natural stone that is cut directly from
quarries, quartz countertops are manufactured to achieve consistent patterns,
colors, and finishes, making them ideal for a customized look.

### How Are Quartz Countertops Made?

The process involves blending crushed quartz with resin binders, then forming
the mixture into slabs through high-pressure manufacturing. The result? A
solid surface that boasts the natural beauty of stone but with added strength
and resilience.

### Daily Maintenance Tips

### Long-term Care

## Comparing Quartz with Other Countertop Materials

### Quartz vs. Granite

While both are durable, quartz is more resistant to stains and scratches than
granite because of its non-porous surface, reducing the need for sealing.

### Quartz vs. Marble

Marble exudes elegance but is softer and more prone to etching. Quartz offers
similar beauty with enhanced durability.

### Quartz vs. Laminate

Laminate is budget-friendly but less durable and susceptible to scratches and
burns. Quartz provides longevity and a luxurious look.
