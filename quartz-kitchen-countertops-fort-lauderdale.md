## **What We Mean by “Quartz Kitchen Countertops”**

Let’s clear up a common confusion: quartz countertops are engineered stone
made from natural quartz and resins. They’re not “plastic,” and they’re not
“fake.” Think of it like a performance car: engineered for precision and
reliability.

### **Quartz vs. Quartzite**

Quartzite is a natural stone. Quartz is engineered. Both can be gorgeous—but
they behave differently.

#### **Our Rule of Thumb**

If you want low maintenance, quartz wins. If you want wild, natural movement
and you’re okay with more upkeep, quartzite might be worth exploring.

## **Caring for Quartz Kitchen Countertops**

Quartz is low maintenance, not no maintenance. The good news? It’s easy.

### **Daily Cleaning**

Warm water + mild soap is usually all you need.

### **Heat and Quartz**

Quartz is durable, but we recommend trivets for hot pans. Think of it like
sunscreen: you might not “need” it every day, but you’ll be glad you used it.

#### **Avoid Harsh Chemicals**

Skip abrasive cleaners and harsh solvents. Gentleness is better.

## **FAQs**

### **1) Are quartz kitchen countertops good for heavy cooking?**

Yes. Quartz is highly durable and non-porous, making it great for busy
kitchens—just use trivets for hot pans.

### **2) Do quartz countertops stain easily?**

No. Quartz resists stains well because it’s non-porous, especially compared to
many natural stones.

### **3) Do I need to seal quartz countertops?**

No sealing is required for quartz, which is one reason it’s so popular for
kitchen countertops.

### **4) Can quartz look like marble?**

Absolutely. Many quartz designs offer elegant veining that mimics marble
without the etching risk.

### **5) What affects the cost of quartz kitchen countertops the most?**

Material tier, square footage, cutouts, edge profiles, and seam complexity are
usually the biggest factors.
